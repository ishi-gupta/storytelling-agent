"Shaw," O'Hara said, slapping a manila folder onto the messy desk. "Got a new case for you."

Shaw glanced at the folder before returning his gaze to the form he was filling out. "I'm swamped, Captain. Can't it wait?"

"It's the Rutherford Mansion," O'Hara replied, ignoring Shaw's protest. His voice held an odd mix of excitement and dread, a combination that made Shaw look up.

"The old haunted place on the outskirts?" Shaw asked, raising an eyebrow. "What's the case? Kids breaking in and scaring themselves silly?"

O'Hara shook his head. "Not this time. This one's different. We found a body."

Shaw leaned back in his chair, crossing his arms. "So? Let Homicide handle it. Why me?"

"Because," O'Hara said, leaning forward on Shaw's desk, "This isn't just any body. This is Amelia Rutherford."

The name hung in the air between them. Amelia Rutherford, the heiress who had vanished without a trace fifty years ago, was a legend in their small town. Shaw let out a sigh, rubbing his temples. "And you want me to take the case? After all these years?"

O'Hara nodded. "Yes, Shaw, I do. You're the best we got, and this case... It's going to be big. Might even make national news. I need you on this."

Shaw sat silent for a moment, his gaze drifting to the manila folder. He didn't want this case. He didn't need the stress, the media circus, or the headache that would undoubtedly come with it. But he also knew that O'Hara was right. He was the best they had, and if anyone could solve this mystery, it was him.

With a resigned sigh, Shaw grabbed the folder and opened it, scanning the initial report. "Fine," he said, meeting O'Hara's gaze. "I'll take the case. But I'm doing it my way."

O'Hara grinned, clapping Shaw on the shoulder. "That's all I ask, Shaw. That's all I ask."

As O'Hara walked away, Shaw looked down at the folder again, his interest piqued despite himself. The Rutherford Mansion case. He had a feeling it was going to be a long few weeks.
